# Cover Letter Templates (JAMA / Lancet / Nature Medicine)

**Target length**: 350-450 words (5 paragraphs)
**Reading time**: 5 min

📖 **See**: [Stage 10 Pre-Submission Checklist](../../CLAUDE.md#stage-10-pre-submission-final-checklist) for submission workflow

---

## Universal 5-Paragraph Structure

All 3 journals follow this format:

1. **Hook + Research Gap** (3-4 sentences) — Clinical problem + evidence gap
2. **Study Novelty** (3-4 sentences) — First NMA, unique contribution, rigor
3. **Clinical Impact** (2-3 sentences) — Practice changes, patient benefits
4. **Journal Fit** (2-3 sentences) — Why this journal, alignment with mission
5. **Compliance** (2 sentences) — PROSPERO, authorship, conflicts

## Example: JAMA Oncology (from early-immuno-timing-nma)

```markdown
Dear Editor,

We submit "Optimal Timing of Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor Therapy in Resectable Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer: A Bayesian Network Meta-Analysis" for consideration as an Original Investigation in JAMA Oncology. Immune checkpoint inhibitors improve survival in resectable non-small cell lung cancer, yet the optimal timing strategy (perioperative, neoadjuvant-only, or adjuvant-only) remains undefined due to the absence of head-to-head trials. Individual trials demonstrate efficacy for each strategy, but clinicians lack quantitative evidence to guide timing decisions. This knowledge gap leaves oncologists without evidence-based guidance for selecting between 12-week perioperative therapy versus 12-month adjuvant-only approaches.

This is the first Bayesian network meta-analysis to compare all three ICI timing strategies with probabilistic rankings. Using data from 10 phase 3 randomized clinical trials (N=9,907 patients), we demonstrate perioperative ICI reduces event-free survival events by 44% compared with adjuvant-only (HR 0.565, 87.8% probability best) and 21% compared with neoadjuvant-only (HR 0.794, 11.2% probability best). We explain the paradoxical overall survival findings through methodological analysis of follow-up maturity (perioperative trials: 25-38 months vs adjuvant: 60 months) rather than speculative biological mechanisms.

Our four scenario-based clinical guidance sections translate findings into actionable treatment pathway recommendations, addressing shared decision-making for patients with surgical delays, neoadjuvant contraindications, adjuvant intolerance, and uncertain surgical candidacy. This directly addresses JAMA Oncology's mission of advancing patient-centered oncology research through rigorous comparative effectiveness evidence.

This work aligns with recent JAMA Oncology publications on perioperative immunotherapy (Forde et al. 2025) and extends the evidence base through the first quantitative ranking of all timing strategies.

This review was registered on PROSPERO (CRD42026123456). All authors approved the manuscript. We declare no conflicts of interest or funding.

We believe this work will interest JAMA Oncology readers and welcome peer review.

Sincerely,
Jane Doe, MD, PhD
Associate Professor of Medical Oncology
University of XYZ
```

**Word count**: 345 words ✅
